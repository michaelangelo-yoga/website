The Practice of Yoga

Yoga is more than movement. It is a path of awareness, connection, and care that supports every layer of your being.

What Is Yoga?

At its heart, yoga is the practice of uniting body, breath, and mind. The word itself comes from the Sanskrit root yuj, meaning to yoke or join.

Modern yoga classes often focus on physical postures (asana), but the tradition is far broader. It includes breathwork (pranayama), ethical principles, concentration, meditation, and self-inquiry.

In my classes, we honor the full tradition while keeping things accessible. You do not need to be flexible, strong, or spiritual to begin. You only need a willingness to show up and breathe.

Styles You May Encounter

Hatha

A steady, alignment-based practice that introduces foundational poses and breathing techniques. Great for building confidence and body awareness.

Vinyasa

Movement synchronized with breath, often flowing through sequences like sun salutations. Energetic, creative, and adaptable.

Restorative

Long, supported holds with props to encourage deep relaxation and nervous system regulation. A balm for modern life.

Yin

Slow, passive stretching of connective tissues over several minutes. Develops flexibility and patience while quieting the mind.

Breathwork & Meditation

Practices that train attention, regulate the nervous system, and create space between stimulus and response.

Functional Movement

A modern approach that prioritizes mobility, stability, and healthy joint patterns over aesthetic shapes.
